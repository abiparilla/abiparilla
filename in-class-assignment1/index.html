Save the Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are an endangered species, with over one million killed each year by ingesting plastic and other forms of debris. It is crucial that we take action to save sea turtles from extinction.

Why Are Sea Turtles Important?

  • Helps to maintain the health of coral reefs and seagrass beds
  • Essential for coastal economies and native communities
  • Hatchlings are an important source of food for many animals

How can I help save them?

Reduce plastic use

  • Use reusable bags, bottles, and straws
  • Recycle plastic
  • Avoid releasing balloons into coastal areas

Protect nesting beaches

  • Avoid walking on the beach at night during nesting season
  • Don't use flashlights or bright lights near nesting turtles
  • Stay away from marked sea turtle nests

If you see an entangled, stranded, sick, injured, or dead animal, please report it by calling your local stranding network or by using the NOAA Fisheries' app: Dolphin & Whale 911
